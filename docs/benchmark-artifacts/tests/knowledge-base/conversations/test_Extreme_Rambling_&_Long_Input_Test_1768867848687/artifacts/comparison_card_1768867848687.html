Decision Point: Max's Game vs. Investor Call
Prioritizing Saturday
Attend Max's Game
  • Fulfills promise to Max, reinforcing trust and your presence.
  • Supports Max's participation and enjoyment in his activity.
  • Reduces guilt and potential disappointment for Max.
  • Provides a needed break and connection with your son.
  • Potential conflict with a crucial investor call.
  • Risk of missing an opportunity for funding or a key connection.
  • Could be perceived as a lack of commitment to the startup by investors (if call is missed/rescheduled poorly).
Prioritize Investor Call
  • Direct engagement with potential funding source.
  • Demonstrates commitment and seriousness to investors.
  • Potential to secure vital funding for the startup's future.
  • Avoids negative perception from investors regarding availability.
  • Max's disappointment and potential feeling of not being a priority.
  • Missed opportunity to support Max in a significant event.
  • Increased guilt and stress from breaking a promise.
  • Potential need to reschedule the investor call, which may not be possible or ideal.
Consider if the investor call can be rescheduled or if a compromise is possible (e.g., attending part of the game, having Jordan attend the whole game).
Decision Point: Emma's Reading & Testing
Supporting Emma's Growth
Pursue Formal Testing
  • Provides a clear understanding of Emma's specific learning profile.
  • Identifies potential learning differences or strengths.
  • Offers data-driven strategies for support (e.g., IEP, specific interventions).
  • Can alleviate your worry by providing concrete next steps.
  • Potential for Emma to feel labeled or "different."
  • Risk of focusing too much on deficits rather than strengths.
  • Can be an emotional process for both Emma and you.
  • May lead to increased pressure if not managed carefully.
Focus on Strategies & Strengths (No Formal Testing Yet)
  • Maintains Emma's self-esteem by avoiding labels.
  • Leverages current successes (like graphic novels) and builds on them.
  • Allows for a more organic approach to support.
  • Less immediate pressure and emotional intensity.
  • May miss underlying issues that testing could reveal.
  • Could lead to continued struggles if a specific learning difference is present.
  • Less objective data to guide interventions, potentially leading to less effective strategies.
  • Might delay more targeted support if needed.
Consider discussing testing with Ms. Johnson to understand the process and potential benefits/drawbacks from her perspective. Focus on framing any support as helping Emma learn in the way that works best for *her*.
